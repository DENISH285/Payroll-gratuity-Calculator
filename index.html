How this is worked out

  • Gratuity is generally payable after 5 years of continuous service. This requirement is waived if employment ends due to death or disablement from accident or disease.
  • Several High Courts have held that completing 4 years plus 240 or more working days in the fifth year also satisfies the 5-year requirement under Section 2A. This is a court-recognized position, not explicit statutory text, so it's worth confirming with your employer.
  • Fixed-term employees qualify after 1 year of continuous service under the Code on Social Security, 2020.
  • The formula is 15 days' wages for each year of service: (last drawn salary × 15 × years) ÷ 26 for employers covered under the Act, or ÷ 30 otherwise. "Salary" means basic pay plus dearness allowance.
  • If your final year of service runs 6 months or more past your last full year, it's rounded up to a full year.
  • Gratuity is capped at ₹20 lakh for private-sector employees and ₹25 lakh for central government employees; amounts above this aren't tax-exempt even if an employer chooses to pay more.

This calculator gives an estimate for general guidance only and isn't a substitute for your employer's payroll or HR computation, or for legal advice.
